💀 Hall of Fallen Startups

Real ideas. Real destruction. Learn from their deaths.

← Kill your own idea
🐕 Uber for Dogs
CAUSE OF DEATH: Unit economics impossible at any scale
"The investor destroyed it in seconds — the average dog walk order is $15, drivers cost $12, insurance adds $8. You lose money on every single ride. There is no path to profitability, only a path to bankruptcy."
💀 Died in: 4 months
📚 Netflix for Books
CAUSE OF DEATH: Amazon Kindle Unlimited already exists
"The competitor agent was brutal — Amazon launched Kindle Unlimited in 2014. It has 4 million books, 3 million subscribers, and loses money intentionally to kill exactly you. You have $50k. They have $500 billion."
💀 Died in: 2 months
🤖 AI Lawyer
CAUSE OF DEATH: Regulatory shutdown + liability
"The regulator killed it immediately — providing legal advice without a license is illegal in every US state. One wrong answer and you face a $250,000 fine and criminal charges. The Bar Association is already lobbying against AI legal tools."
💀 Died in: 1 month
🏠 Airbnb for Offices
CAUSE OF DEATH: WeWork tried this with $12B and still failed
"The pessimist analyst noted that post-COVID office occupancy is at 48% and declining. WeWork burned $12 billion proving this doesn't work. Remote work is permanent. Your timing is 5 years too late."
💀 Died in: 6 months
💊 Subscription Vitamins
CAUSE OF DEATH: Customer churn kills subscription health businesses
"The lazy customer explained it perfectly — I subscribed to Care/of, Athletic Greens, and Ritual. I cancelled all three within 3 months. Vitamins are boring. I forget to take them. I don't see results. Nobody renews."
💀 Died in: 8 months
🍕 Ghost Kitchen Platform
CAUSE OF DEATH: Margins of -40% with no path to positive
"The burnt-out employee described month 7 — we had 12 kitchens, zero were profitable, the founder kept saying 'scale fixes margins.' It doesn't. Food costs 35%, delivery 30%, rent 20%. You need 85% margins to survive. Food gives you 15%."
💀 Died in: 11 months
